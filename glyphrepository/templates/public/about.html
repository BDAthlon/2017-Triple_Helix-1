About

Over the past 20 years, the visual representation of genetic sequence features, parts, and constructs has evolved considerably, from simple colored blocks to a variety of glyphs capturing diï¬€erent sequence functions. During this time, organizations such as the Synthetic Biology Open Language (SBOL) Developers Group have made considerable progress in cataloguing commonly used synthetic biology glyphs and establishing standard procedures for curating new ones, resulting in the SBOL visual 1.0.0 standard, and the publication SBOL Visual: A Graphical Language for Genetic Designs. This SBOLv standard is supported by several tools, including VisBOL, SBOL Designer, PigeonCAD, and the DNAplotlib library.

What the greater synthetic biology community still lacks, however, is a common online resource for aggregating and distributing data for genetic design visualization. The SBOL visual symbols are stored in a Github repository, rather than a dedicated browser. Whilst the SBOLv project is minimally prescriptive, it is still directed towards forming consensus recommendations, rather than documenting the diversity of usage, and there are aspects diagramming practice that it has not yet fully considered.

The SynBio Glyphs Repository was created to be as such a resource by James Scott-Brown, Iulia Gherman and Margarita Kopniczky at the BDAthlon during the 2017 International Workshop on Bio-Design Auotmation in Pittsburgh.
